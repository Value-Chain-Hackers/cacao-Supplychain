Overview of Cacao

Cacao (Theobroma cacao) is a tropical tree native to the deep tropical regions of the Americas. Its seeds, called cacao beans, are the main ingredient in chocolate production.

The cacao tree produces large, pod-like fruits, each containing 20 to 60 beans surrounded by a sweet, white pulp. These beans undergo a complex journey before becoming the chocolate we enjoy.

Cacao has been cultivated for at least three millennia in Mexico, Central America, and Northern South America. Today, about 70% of the world's cacao is grown in West Africa, with Ivory Coast being the largest producer.

Challenges in the Cacao Supply Chain

Farmer Income

Many cacao farmers live in poverty, earning less than $1 per day despite chocolate being a $100+ billion industry.

Child Labor

Child labor remains a significant issue in some cacao-growing regions, particularly in West Africa.

Deforestation

Cacao farming has contributed to deforestation in sensitive ecological areas, particularly in West Africa and Indonesia.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and changing rainfall patterns threaten cacao-growing regions, potentially reducing suitable growing areas.

Price Volatility

Cacao prices are highly volatile, making it difficult for farmers to plan and invest in their farms.

Aging Farmer Population

The average age of cacao farmers is increasing, with fewer young people choosing farming as a profession.

Innovations and Improvements

Sustainability Initiatives

Major chocolate companies have committed to sourcing 100% sustainable cacao by various target dates, investing in farmer training and community development.

Certification Programs

Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, and UTZ certification programs help ensure better practices and prices for farmers.

Direct Trade

Some chocolate makers bypass traditional supply chains to work directly with farmers, ensuring higher prices and better quality.

Technology Adoption

Mobile apps, blockchain technology, and satellite monitoring are being used to increase transparency and traceability in the supply chain.

Research and Development

Research into disease-resistant, high-yield cacao varieties helps farmers improve productivity and resilience.

Agroforestry Systems

Growing cacao alongside other crops and trees can improve biodiversity, soil health, and provide additional income sources for farmers.
